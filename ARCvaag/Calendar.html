Calendar:

A calendar is a system of organizing days for social, religious, commercial, or administrative purposes. It helps to manage time by dividing years, months, weeks, and days. Let’s break down the essentials of calendars, focusing on how they’re used in reasoning and competitive exams.

Key Concepts in Calendar Problems

1.Days of the Week:
#. The days follow a fixed sequence: Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday. #. Calendar questions often require finding the day of the week on a given date
Leap Year: 2.A year is a leap year if: #.It is divisible by 4, but not by 100, or #.It is divisible by 400. #.Leap years have 366 days (with February 29), while regular years have 365 days. #.Every leap year has 52 weeks and 2 extra days, and every non-leap year has 52 weeks and 1 extra day.
3.Number of Odd Days Definition: Odd days refer to the extra days left after dividing a period by 7. This concept helps determine the day of the week for any given date. 1.1 ordinary year = 365 days = 52 weeks + 1 day → 1 odd day 2.1 leap year = 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 days → 2 odd days 3.100 years = 76 ordinary years + 24 leap years = 36524 days = 5 odd days 4.200 years = 10 odd days → 3 odd days (as 10 mod 7 is 3) 5.300 years = 15 odd days → 1 odd day 6.400 years = 0 odd days (as it’s exactly divisible by 7)

1. Action Motive:

In this type of statement and conclusion reasoning, questions are based on an action being undertaken. If such an action is always taken with a particular type of motive, then such type of motive exists.

2. Cause Effect:

In this type of statement and conclusion reasoning, questions are based on the type of effects, which would take place for a particular cause. If an action is undertaken and if such an action always leads to a particular type of effect, then it is definitely true and such an effect would take place.

3. Keywords:

One of the most important aspects of evaluating this type of statement and conclusion reasoning is to understand some popular words, which are known as keywords. The most significant use of keywords is that it helps in distinguishing between definitely true, probably true, and definitely false statements.
In Standard Keywords, adjectives, conjunctions, verbs, and adverbs, which are used in the statement provide the idea on the quantity as well as the quality of a statement. Some examples are, some, none, never, always, sometimes, must be, etc.

5. Formula to Find the Day of the Week

To find the day of the week for any given date, use the following steps: Calculate the Day Code:
Day Code= (Date + Month Code + Year Code + Century Code) mod 7
Breakdown of Components: a.Date: The day of the month.
b. Month Code: As per the table above.
c. Year Code: The last two digits of the year
d. Century Code: Based on the century (from the Century Code table).
e. Adjust for Leap Years: If the date is in January or February of a leap year, subtract 1 from the total.
