DIRECTIONS

In reasoning, "direction" refers to the ability to determine spatial orientation, particularly the relative positions or movements of objects or persons. Direction-based reasoning problems test an individual's ability to understand and interpret directional cues and navigate accordingly. They often involve scenarios where a person or object moves in specific directions (north, south, east, west) or rotates to face a different orientation.
These problems generally require interpreting complex movements and changes in direction, understanding relative positioning, and keeping track of multiple turns or shifts in location. To solve them, one must visualize or diagram the scenario, maintain a mental map, and consistently apply directional cues. Direction-based reasoning is essential in fields like navigation, military strategy, logistics, and urban planning, where spatial awareness and the ability to quickly assess directions are crucial.
Typically, problems in this category involve:
- Determining the final direction or location after a series of turns.
- Establishing distances covered in different directions.
- Visualizing compass-based directions for orientation-related questions.

Assumptions Related to Direction Reasoning
The main direction change undergoes a 90° change in direction. For instance, there is a 90° change from East to North / South.
The change is only 45° change in cardinal direction. For example, there is a 45° change in North to North – East or North to North – West.
The direction of the right turn is always clockwise.
The direction of the left turn is always anti-clockwise.
The problem on the distance covered or the minimal distance between two points in direction reasoning is solved by using the concept of Pythagoras theorem.
All these distances are along straight lines and between specified points.

Tipcs and Tricks

There are only four primary directions: north, south, east and west.
1. North-East, North-West, South-East, and South-West are the four cardinal directions.
2. If a man stands facing east at sunrise, his shadow will be cast towards the west.
3. The shadow of an item is always in the east during sunset.
4. If a guy stands facing North at daybreak, his shadow will be to his left, and at sunset, it will be to his right.
5. The sun’s beams are vertically downward at 12:00 noon, therefore there will be no shadow..
