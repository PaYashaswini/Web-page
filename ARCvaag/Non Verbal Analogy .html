NonVerbal-Analogy:

Non-Verbal Analogy problems involve identifying relationships between images, shapes, patterns, or figures instead of words. These analogies test spatial and visual reasoning skills, where the task is to analyze how two images or patterns relate and then find a corresponding relationship between a new pair.
Typical non-verbal analogy questions might include:
1.Rotations or reflections of shapes
2.Identifying similar visual patterns
3.Completing sequences or selecting a shape that fits.

Topics in non-verbal reasoning typically include:

1.Analogies: Finding relationships between figures or shapes.
2.Series Completion: Identifying patterns to complete a sequence.
3.Classification: Grouping similar images or figures.
4.Mirror and Water Images: Reflecting figures vertically or horizontally.
5.Figure Matrix: Solving figure-based puzzles arranged in matrices.
6.Embedded and Hidden Figures: Finding a shape within a complex figure.
7.Paper Folding and Cutting: Visualizing effects of folding and cutting paper.
8.Pattern Completion: Completing patterns based on symmetry or continuation.
